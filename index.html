What is PAR Scoring?

The Peer Assessed Rating (PAR) index is the most widely accepted system for analysing a patient’s orthodontic movement and how much they have progressed. The index is split into the following five categories: upper and lower anterior segments, left and right buccal occlusion, overjet, overbite/open bite and centreline. Within each of these categories the person scoring must attribute scores to various aspects of the patient’s dentition. Ideal positioning and alignment would result in a score of zero. Whereas crowding, spacing, poor interdigitation of upper and lower teeth, overjet, crossbite, overbite, open bite and misaligned centrelines could all result in a higher score. Dentists and orthodontists are required to submit 20 cases plus 10% of their remaining caseload per year for PAR scoring.
